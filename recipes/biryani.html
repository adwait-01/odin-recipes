Veg Biryani

Home

Description:

Veg Biryani is a delicious and aromatic rice dish made with basmati rice, mixed vegetables, spices, and herbs. It is a vegetarian version of the popular biryani and is known for its rich flavors, vibrant colors, and layered texture.

This dish is typically cooked in layers, with rice and vegetables cooked separately and then layered together with spices, fried onions, and sometimes saffron or rose water for added aroma. It is best enjoyed with raita, salad, or pickle.

Ingredients

  • For Rice:
    • Basmati rice
    • Water
    • Salt
    • Bay leaf
    • Cloves
    • Cardamom
    • Cinnamon stick
  • For Veggie Masala:
    • Mixed vegetables (carrot, beans, peas, potatoes)
    • Onion (sliced)
    • Tomato (chopped)
    • Green chili (optional)
    • Ginger-Garlic paste
    • Yogurt
    • Mint leaves
    • Coriander leaves
    • Biryani masala or garam masala
    • Red chili powder
    • Turmeric powder
    • Salt
    • Oil or ghee
    • Fried onions (optional)

Steps:

  • Step 1: Cook the Rice
    1. Rinse and soak basmati rice for 20-30 minutes.
    2. Boil water with salt, bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, and cinnamon.
    3. Add soaked rice and cook until 90% done. Drain and set aside.
  • Step 2: Prepare the Veggie Masala
    1. Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
    2. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chili. Sauté briefly.
    3. Add tomatoes and cook until soft.
    4. Add mixed vegetables and cook for a few minutes.
    5. Add yogurt, biryani masala, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
    6. Add mint and coriander leaves. Cook until veggies are tender and masala is well combined.
  • Step 3: Layer and Cook
    1. In a large pot or handi, add a layer of cooked veggies.
    2. Add a layer of rice over the veggies.
    3. Repeat layers and top with fried onions, mint, and coriander (optional: saffron milk).
    4. Cover tightly and cook on low heat (dum) for 10-15 minutes.
    5. Let it rest for a few minutes, then serve hot with raita.
